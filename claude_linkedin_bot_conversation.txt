● Here's what changed and why it makes style work:

  The core problem was that Claude had abstract style rules ("use punchy
  openings") but no concrete post to emulate. LLMs are dramatically better at
  style transfer from examples than from descriptions.

  What's new:

  Layer: System prompt
  Before: Style card (abstract rules)
  After: Style card + 5 real Roman posts embedded directly — Claude always has
    authentic examples in context
  ────────────────────────────────────────
  Layer: Retrieved posts
  Before: 5 raw texts in JSON array
  After: 8 posts with similarity % + explicit instruction "copy this opening
    structure, paragraph rhythm, and closing pattern"
  ────────────────────────────────────────
  Layer: Writing instruction
  Before: "write in his style"
  After: Step-by-step: pick the closest example → identify its structure → write

    with new content in same mold

  If the style is still off after testing, the most powerful lever is to
  manually curate example_posts.json — open it and replace any weak examples
  with 5 of Roman's best posts. The script picks by topic keywords and length,
  but you know better which posts are most "him."
